## Buying Guide

### Mounting Styles and Frame Compatibility
The most secure cages bolt directly into pre-drilled, threaded frame inserts (braze-ons). If your bike or scooter lacks these holes, you will need a clamp-on style or a fabric strap mount. Always check the required bolt spacing for hard-mounted cages; while standard spacing is typically around 2.5 inches, slight variations can make installation impossible without modification. For clamp-on designs, verify the maximum clamp diameter against your handlebars or frame tubes to prevent buying a mount that will not close securely.

### Material Tradeoffs and Bottle Care
Aluminum cages allow you to bend the arms slightly for a tighter grip, but they are notorious for scratching the paint off metal vacuum-insulated bottles. Polycarbonate and plastic cages will not scratch metal bottles and are often lighter, but they can become brittle in extreme cold or snap under heavy impacts. Fabric cages with velcro straps offer the most versatility for odd-shaped containers and will not scratch your gear, but they add bulk and lack the fast, one-handed sliding action of rigid cages.

### Weight Limits and Vibration Risk
Clamp-on cages and plastic brackets often carry strict weight limits. For example, some no-drill clamps max out at around 27 ounces of water. Filling a massive steel thermos and clamping it to your handlebars shifts your steering weight and strains the clamp material. If you ride aggressive trails or washboard gravel, heavy bottles in clamp-style mounts are highly likely to wobble, slip, or snap off. Reserve clamp mounts for casual riding and hard-mounted alloy or heavy-duty fabric cages for off-road recreation.

## Common Questions Before Buying

### What should I check before buying a bike bottle cage?

Check bottle diameter support, frame mount compatibility, retention grip, included bolts, and whether the cage is easy to access while riding.

### Do all water bottle cages fit every bike?

No. Most use standard frame bosses, but small frames, full-suspension bikes, and oversized bottles can create clearance problems.

### What bottle cage complaints matter most?

Bottles rattling loose, weak bolts, sharp edges, and cages that crack or bend are the problems most likely to show up during rides.
